# Steampowered.com Evolution: A Text-Based Analysis (2002-2009)

This document analyzes the textual content of steampowered.com from 2002 to 2009, based on snapshots from the Internet Archive. While the full HTML and CSS could not be retrieved, this analysis of the text content provides valuable insights into the site's evolution.

## 2002

- **Focus:** Primarily a corporate and developer-facing platform.
- **Key Messaging:** "Steam is a broadband business platform for direct software delivery and content management."
- **Content:** Technical descriptions of Steam's features (distributed file system, anti-cheating, anti-piracy), press releases, and contact information for business inquiries.
- **Navigation:** "Try Steam Now!", "Steam Support Site", "Valve Home"

## 2003

- **Focus:** Shift towards a consumer-facing platform.
- **Key Messaging:** "Become *PART OF THE STEAM COMMUNITY*"
- **Content:** Highlights of consumer features (automatic updates, in-game chat, server browsing), news about game updates (Counter-Strike, Day of Defeat), and a more user-friendly explanation of what Steam is.
- **Navigation:** Links to install Steam, feature lists, and privacy policy.

## 2004

- **Focus:** Growth of the platform and the introduction of Half-Life 2.
- **Key Messaging:** "Sign Up and Play Games Today!"
- **Content:** Prominent advertising for Half-Life 2 packages (Bronze, Silver, Gold), news about Half-Life 2: Deathmatch, and information for cyber cafes.
- **Navigation:** Clear calls to action ("PLAY IT NOW", "install it now"), links to support pages, and information about the Cyber Cafe Program.

## 2005

- **Focus:** Expansion of the game library and community features.
- **Key Messaging:** "Welcome to Steam"
- **Content:** News about new game releases (Darwinia), updates to the Source engine and Half-Life 1 engine, and information about tournaments and LAN parties.
- **Navigation:** Links to buy games, official game sites, the Valve store, and community/support pages.

## 2006, 2007 & 2008

- **Content:** The extracted text for these years is primarily from the Internet Archive's own interface. However, a key piece of information was found: a redirect from `steampowered.com` to `store.steampowered.com` was in place by 2008, and likely earlier. This indicates a major shift in the platform's strategy, with a clear separation between the corporate site and the consumer-facing storefront.

## 2009

- **Content:** Similar to 2006 and 2007, the extracted text is mostly from the Internet Archive's interface. However, it does indicate a significant change: a redirect from `steampowered.com` to `store.steampowered.com`.

## Summary of Evolution

- **2002-2003:** Shift from a B2B platform to a B2C gaming community.
- **2004:** Major focus on the release of Half-Life 2 and driving user adoption.
- **2005:** Expansion of the game catalog and the introduction of more community-oriented features.
- **2006-2008:** (Data missing) Likely a period of significant growth and feature expansion, leading to the dedicated storefront.
- **2009:** The emergence of the dedicated Steam Store, marking a major milestone in the platform's evolution.

This text-based analysis provides a foundational understanding of how steampowered.com evolved. The next step will be to attempt to find visual references for these years to complement this textual data.
